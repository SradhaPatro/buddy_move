## PART 2: Product Definition

### One Sentence Definition
MoveBuddy is a trusted commute platform that matches verified daily riders with vetted hosts in a subscription-driven shared ride ecosystem.

### One Paragraph Definition
MoveBuddy is a commuter-first mobility platform that enables guests to book recurring daily rides and hosts to activate their vehicle capacity through a secure, subscription-based marketplace. It combines smart matching, transparent pricing, real-time ride tracking, wallet payments, and safety workflows to deliver a dependable daily commute experience that is simpler than ride-hailing and safer than informal carpooling.

### One Page Definition
MoveBuddy is not a generic taxi or delivery app. It is a daily commute network optimized for repeated morning and evening trips, where guests subscribe for consistent access and hosts activate their vehicle capacity for reliable earnings. The platform focuses on route-based matching, commute direction logic, and predictable schedules rather than on-demand taxi dispatch.

MoveBuddy is built around two primary user roles: guests who need a dependable ride to work, school, or college, and hosts who can offer seats on repeat routes. Each ride is matched through route overlap, time tolerance, and capacity rules that respect daily commute rhythms. Pricing is subscription-centric, meaning guests pay for curated commute access rather than transactional per-ride costs. Hosts earn through activation and ride completion, with safeguards that require verification and accountability.

MoveBuddy is designed for safety, with KYC and verification workflows, SOS mechanisms, and a ride lifecycle that tracks from creation through completion. Its business model is anchored in recurring subscriptions, host activation fees, wallet-backed transactions, and future enterprise partnerships for campus and corporate commuting. The product is intentionally not Uber, Ola, Rapido, or BlaBlaCar. It does not compete with on-demand taxis or casual carpooling. Instead, it occupies a new category of structured daily commute sharing built for trust and reliability.

### Explain why MoveBuddy is NOT:

- **Uber**: MoveBuddy is not an on-demand taxi service. It is focused on recurring commute routes, subscription membership, and ride sharing on a fixed schedule rather than instant car-hire.
- **Ola**: MoveBuddy is not a ride-hailing marketplace for arbitrary trips. It is a shared commute service that matches guests to hosts on daily routes with subscription pricing, not dynamic surge-based short rides.
- **Rapido**: MoveBuddy is not a bike taxi or intra-city on-demand delivery service. It is built for shared seat-based commuting with route matching and host activation, not solo two-wheeler hires.
- **BlaBlaCar**: MoveBuddy is not long-distance intercity carpooling. It is a daily commute offering for local and regional travel, optimized for morning and evening commute patterns, with subscription and host activation built-in.
- **Traditional Carpooling**: MoveBuddy is not informal, unverified carpools. It provides an app-managed, verified, payment-backed system with admin oversight, KYC, wallet controls, and emergency support.

### What makes MoveBuddy unique
- Subscription-first model for daily commute access
- Verified host and guest ecosystem with KYC and safety controls
- Route-based matching tuned for morning/evening commute cycles
- Wallet-backed payments and recurring ride lifecycle management
- Community trust built on transparency, support, and reliable schedules
- A dual-sided product that supports both guests and hosts with dedicated experiences
- Admin and support workflows to maintain safety, pricing, and operational health

## PART 6: Business Rules

### Guest subscription
- Guests must subscribe before they can book or access ride matches.
- A subscription defines the commute window, route preference, and time tolerance.
- Subscriptions may cover morning, evening, or both directions.
- A guest cannot ride without an active subscription.
- Subscription expiry removes matching eligibility until renewed.

### Host subscription
- Hosts must activate with valid KYC and vehicle details before listing rides.
- Host activation may require a fee and a minimum commitment to recurring service.
- Hosts remain inactive until verification is approved.
- Hosts cannot accept guests beyond their declared capacity.

### Morning commute
- Morning commute is a defined ride direction from home to work/college.
- Matches prioritize hosts whose route covers guest pickup and drop-off within a morning window.
- Guests and hosts must agree on expected arrival times and pickup radius.

### Evening commute
- Evening commute is the return direction from work/college to home.
- Evening route matching is separate from morning matching but follows the same route overlap rules.
- Guests may have different timing needs and route preferences for evening travel.

### Independent matching
- Matching works independently for each commute ride.
- A guest can match with different hosts for different days or directions.
- Repeated good matches are preferred but not guaranteed.

### Same-host preference
- When possible, guests are matched with the same verified host across commute cycles.
- Same-host matching is a preference, not a guarantee, and is based on route, capacity, and reliability.

### RouteSchedule
- Every ride request or host offer includes a route schedule, day pattern, and allowed timing tolerance.
- RouteSchedule must clearly define origin, destination, departure window, and direction.
- The system must respect schedule constraints and not force mismatched pickups.

### Ride generation
- A ride is created when a host confirms an eligible match with a guest.
- A ride consists of a defined pickup, drop-off, time window, and passenger seat.
- Rides are generated at the start of each matching cycle and may be updated if guests or hosts change status.

### Vehicle ownership
- Hosts must declare ownership or legal permission to operate the vehicle.
- Only valid, verified vehicles may be used for MoveBuddy rides.
- Vehicle details are part of KYC and host verification.

### Seat allocation
- Hosts declare total available seats for each commute.
- The system allocates seats to guests up to the host capacity.
- Seats are not overbooked.

### Ride capacity
- Ride capacity is fixed per host offer and cannot be exceeded.
- If demand exceeds supply, matching respects first-come/priority rules based on subscription and route fit.

### Cancellation
- Guests can cancel if permitted by subscription rules and cancellation windows.
- Hosts can cancel only for valid reasons and must notify guests promptly.
- Excessive cancellations by hosts or guests reduce trust scores and may lead to penalties.

### Emergency
- Every ride supports an SOS workflow for immediate escalation.
- Guests and hosts can initiate emergency support from within the ride experience.
- Emergency incidents trigger prioritized support and appropriate follow-up.

### Ride completion
- A ride is marked complete when the guest reaches the destination and the host confirms completion.
- Completion may require verification steps such as check-ins or status updates.
- Completed rides contribute to wallet earnings, rewards, and reliability metrics.

### Wallet
- MoveBuddy uses a wallet model for guest payments and host earnings.
- Guests preload wallet value or pay via payment gateway for subscriptions.
- Hosts receive wallet earnings for completed rides and may request payouts.
- Wallet transactions are recorded transparently.

### Rewards
- Rewards are applied as wallet credits, referral bonuses, or loyalty incentives.
- Rewards are granted for verified ride completion, renewals, and referrals.
- Rewards cannot be redeemed for cash unless explicitly defined in future enterprise agreements.

### Referral
- Referral credits are awarded when a referred user completes a subscription and verified ride.
- Referral benefits are clearly defined and visible to both referring and referred users.

### Pricing
- Pricing is subscription-driven and transparent.
- Guest plan pricing is based on commute period, route distance, and service level.
- Host activation pricing is based on verification, route quality, and host reliability.
- Pricing changes are communicated ahead of time through the app.

### Subscription expiry
- Expired subscriptions immediately remove matching eligibility.
- Expiry triggers notifications to guests and offers renewal options.
- Guests may retain historical ride data but cannot book new rides until renewed.

### Everything
- Every business rule must be explicit and measurable.
- Rules should be enforced in the product experience and through admin operations.
- Rules should be auditable and available for support review.

## PART 8: Ride Lifecycle

### Ride Created
A ride is created when a guest subscription and route preference match a host offer. It becomes a confirmed commute instance with a scheduled pickup, drop-off, and assigned passenger seat.

### Matching
Matching evaluates route overlap, time windows, seat availability, and commuting direction. It prefers recurring guest-host pairings when fit and reliability are strong.

### Host Acceptance
A host accepts the match based on route details and capacity. Acceptance unlocks the ride for the guest and confirms the commute plan.

### Guest Confirmation
The guest confirms the assigned ride or may accept an alternate offer. Confirmation signals intent to board and reserves their seat.

### Arrival
The host arrives at the pickup point within the agreed window. The guest is notified and can communicate any delay.

### QR Verification
When available, a verification step confirms guest identity and ride validity at boarding. This may be implemented as a check-in or scan process.

### Ride Started
The ride officially starts when the host departs the pickup point with the guest onboard and updates the status.

### Ride In Progress
The ride continues through the route. Both guest and host have access to status updates and support.

### Ride Completed
The ride is marked complete when the guest reaches the destination and the host confirms completion. Wallet credits and host earnings are finalized.

### Emergency
If an emergency occurs at any stage, either party triggers the SOS workflow for immediate support and follow-up. Emergency incidents are prioritized above all routine actions.

### Cancellation
A ride can be cancelled before or during the trip under defined rules. Guests may cancel within permitted windows; hosts may cancel only for valid reasons and should offer alternatives.

### Dispute
If a guest or host disputes the ride outcome, support and admin review the incident with audit logs, ride data, and verification records.

### Rating
After completion, both guest and host provide feedback and ratings. This feeds trust scores and future matching.

### Feedback
Users can share ride experience details, safety concerns, and service suggestions. Feedback is used to improve matching, policies, and operational support.

### Future Loyalty
Completed rides, positive ratings, and consistent subscriptions contribute to loyalty status and potential premium benefits.

## PART 9: Matching Philosophy

### Morning matching
Morning matching focuses on routes from residential origin to work or campus destinations. It values punctual arrival and consistent pickup windows.

### Evening matching
Evening matching focuses on return routes after work or classes. It supports different timing and may have separate host availability than morning matches.

### Independent rides
Each commute direction is treated independently. Guests may match with different hosts in morning and evening, while the system retains continuity where possible.

### Same-host preference
Same-host preference exists to build stability and trust, but it is not mandatory. The system prefers consistent pairings when route fit, timing, and reliability align.

### Route overlap
Matching requires sufficient route overlap between guest origin/destination and host route. Overlap is assessed based on pickup radius, drop-off proximity, and commute direction.

### Distance tolerance
Distance tolerance defines how far guests and hosts can deviate from their core route. It is limited to avoid long detours and preserve commute efficiency.

### Time tolerance
Time tolerance defines acceptable departure and arrival windows. It balances host schedules with guest expectations to prevent late or missed commutes.

### Pickup radius
Pickup radius is the allowed distance between a guest’s location and the host’s route. It must be small enough for practicality and large enough to support matching.

### Seat availability
Matching considers declared host seat capacity and reserves only available seats. Overbooking is prohibited.

### Fairness
Matching should distribute opportunity across hosts and guests in a way that incentivizes reliability and prevents abuse. Preference is given to consistent riders and verified hosts without creating unfair monopolies.

### Reliability
Reliability is a core matching signal. Hosts with strong completion rates and guests with regular subscriptions are prioritized to maintain stable commute experiences.

### Future AI matching
Future enhancements will use AI and ML to optimize route pairings, commuter preferences, historical reliability, and predicted demand while respecting safety and transparency.

### Future ML optimization
Machine learning will improve matching by learning which host-guest combinations deliver the best experience, minimizing cancellations, and optimizing commute network capacity.

## PART 10: Payments Philosophy

### Host activation
Host activation is a paid onboarding or subscription step that creates commitment and funds operational readiness. Activation is required before hosts list commute offers.

### Financial integrity
Financial integrity is non-negotiable. Payments, refunds, wallet adjustments, and host earnings must be accurate, auditable, and aligned with platform rules.

Location data is used only for matching, route planning, and ride status. It is not shared with third parties without consent.

### DPDP principles
MoveBuddy adheres to data protection principles by limiting processing, providing purpose-based use, and enabling rights for correction and deletion.

### Third-party data sharing
Third-party sharing is limited to payment processing, compliance, and safety operations. It requires user consent and legal basis.

### Deletion policy
Users can request deletion of their personal data according to policy. Deletions preserve only what is legally required.

### User rights
Users have rights to access, correct, and delete their data, subject to platform and regulatory requirements.
